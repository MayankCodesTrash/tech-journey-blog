title: "Trying to find what will happen if we remove zoning laws"
date: '2025-07-15'
---

In the late 1800s, during the Gilded Age, the United States followed a "Laissez-faire" approach to land use. They had no zoning laws in place, factories were built directly next to crowded wooden tenements. And The result was a series of catastrophic fires that went through entire city blocks and killed thousands of people because there were no rules to stop the chaos. Today, this bill asks to do the same thing, get rid of our zoning laws.
Today we are debating A Bill to Repeal All Zoning Laws. This legislation isn’t "progress" it is actually a dangerous step backward into a world of unregulated hazards. Because it creates a massive risk of unsafe development, and crushes our local infrastructure, we must vote NO.
My first reason to negate is that this bill creates a massive risk of unsafe and incompatible development. 
• Evidence: According to the American Planning Association (2020), 62% of urban planners report that safety concerns skyrocket when zoning is relaxed.
• Warrant: Zoning is the barrier that keeps hazards away from where children play and sleep.
• Analysis: Section 1 of this bill abolishes all laws that dictate how land is used. This means there is no longer a law stopping a loud, massive shopping mall from opening in the middle of a quiet little residential neighborhood. The Affirmative would point to Section 3C, but it says that the government may regulate land use, this wording is pretty vague and doesn't provide us with what will actually happen.
• Impact: This destroys the quality of life for families. By allowing these buildings to coexist with homes, this bill prioritizes this over the basic survival of American citizens.
My second reason to negate is that this bill puts an impossible problem on our infrastructure.
• Evidence: According to the Lincoln Institute of Land Policy (2021), cities with rapid, unregulated growth experience 25–40% higher utility and road maintenance costs.
• Warrant: You cannot build a 50-story skyscraper on a street designed for five houses without the pipes bursting and the roads failing.
• Analysis: Repealing zoning allows for sudden, huge density in areas that aren't really ready for it. Our sewage systems, power grids, and emergency response routes were built based on zoning plans. If you delete those plans, the infrastructure will fail.
• Impact: This in turn creates a financial nightmare for the middle class. Residents will face higher taxes to fix the broken pipes and clogged roads. Instead of "economic development," we just get a broken system that can’t support the people who live in it. 
This bill is designed to be the "solution" for our housing, but it ignores the bloody lessons of our own history. It trades the safety of our homes for chaos and forces taxpayers to take the bill for the damage. And for these reasons, you must choose the negation on this bill. Thank you.
